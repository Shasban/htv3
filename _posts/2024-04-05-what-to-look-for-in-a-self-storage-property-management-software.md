# What to Look for in a Self-Storage Property Management Software


Operating storage facilities comes with unique management demands - from tracking various unit types and amenities to overseeing maintenance, collections and security. And, without high-functioning centralized software, handling these facility requirements can easily overwhelm your staff.

However, leveraging purpose-built property management software designed specifically around self-storage operational needs allows these businesses to gain control over multifaceted functions. This empowers staff productivity, enhances tenant experiences through systematic processes and also enables data-driven expansion decisions.

Things to look for in a robust storage property management software

When selecting the best self-storage management software, you need to make sure the software has the following features-


### Online Leasing & Rentals

You need to look for a new-age and specially designed [property management software](https://happytenant.io/blog/2023/09/22/revolutionize-property-management-with-cloud-based-software-systems) that makes creating multiple rental listings quick, even with lots of unit types. There should be simply built-in forms to publish spaces online fast. Also this will be nice for leasing multiple units to one customer without repetitive paperwork. Everything should be contracted and billed conveniently in one place. This efficiency means more time focusing on tenant relationships, not repetitive tasks.


### Access Management

Handling security across units, gates and buildings brings challenges. However, the right software has tailored tools just for the complex access needs of the storage owners. Make sure the software allows to set custom permission levels for staff, tenants, vendors - all accessing property at different points. Property managers will know who comes and goes with detailed tracking visibility. Such security control capability takes the hassle out of maintaining safety regulations.


### Automated Billing

Juggling various units on different rental agreements equals billing headaches. But when you select a specialized software, it will centralize all tenant payment data, schedules, invoices - transforming fragmented accounting into one intuitive dashboard. Look for features like automated recurring charges, adjustable invoices and simple payment processing for financial clarity. This oversight helps property managers make strategic decisions to maximize revenue.

### Maintenance Tracking

Monitoring unit doors, climate controls, gates etc - it's extensive for storage sites. However, if you select a maintenance-focused module, it will create full repair history timelines. You will be able to adjust settings to be alerted about pressing issues instantly too. With complete visibility of equipment needs and preventative care, costly emergency fixes drop drastically. Spotting problems early keeps tenants happy and facilities humming.

### Smart Notifications

Lots of things slip between the cracks when staff juggle wide-ranging duties. When you have software with custom notification triggers, it can prevent this by automating important reminders for stuff like overdue payments, closing auctions or expiring vendor agreements to staff's inboxes. Response time improves significantly. The software should also have the facility to create maintenance alerts ensuring repairs happen promptly.

### Inventory Management

Ever struggle to understand occupancy patterns or why certain unit sizes lease faster? Property management software has inventory management tools to analyze all this. Property managers will be able to see availability levels building-to-building to optimize rents. They will be able to identify turnover rates by unit types to refine offerings. These data-driven decisions result in registering reliable growth over time.
