## Compelling Reasons for Developing Strong Relationships with Tenants

**Less Worried About Property Damages**

Broken tiles or damaged furniture are something property owners have to deal with when they are renting out their property. In many cases, tenants who live in a rental apartment don’t bother to fix what they have broken or don’t be careful while using the amenities. But, when your tenants are on good terms with you, they will be more vigilant with everything that they have rented along with the place- whether it is floor, bathroom kitchen amenities or furniture.

**Less Worried About Payments**

Late payments are a serious concern for all property owners and managers. No property wishes to notify their tenants again and again to pay the rent if the tenants exceed the deadline intentionally or unintentionally. The nonchalant behavior of your tenants can be the result of the weak bond between you and your tenants. So, all you have to do is fix your relationship and maintain a solid bond to make sure that your tenants are more enthusiastic to pay the rent on time or at least, inform you of the reason for a late payment and make the payment before the next deadline.

**Less Worried About Termination of Tenancy**

Most property managers fail to retain their tenants for a longer time. So, termination of tenancy is the biggest fear for today’s property managers. As there are hundreds of options available for tenants, it might be challenging to hold your tenants back for a long time. But, it might not be as difficult as you think when your tenants are happy with your approach, behavior, action or services. You can make a list of loyal tenants and minimize the chances of termination of tenancy by investing your time and effort in building a solid rapport with your tenants.
